# Stakeholder Evidence: Methods & Engagement Strategy

## Stakeholder Evidence Goals
**Primary Goal:** To capture stakeholder insights into how cultural integration challenges manifest during M&A, including morale, trust, role clarity, and leadership credibility.
**Secondary Goal:** To gather stakeholder feedback on the effectiveness of integration strategies (e.g., communication, retention programs, governance mechanisms) and identify which approaches stakeholders perceive as most supportive.

## Target Stakeholder Groups

### Internal Stakeholders
#### Management/Leadership
- **Evidence Source:** Executive interviews, press releases, leadership statements in annual reports, analyst commentary.
- **Key Evidence Focus:** How leaders framed the acquisition, their stated priorities (synergies, culture, talent), and credibility signals
- **Data Availability:** High, leadership voices are well-documented in company publications and media

#### Employees/Staff
- **Evidence Source:** Employee testimonials in news articles, case studies, HR practitioner interviews, Glassdoor/LinkedIn commentary (if cited in secondary sources)
- **Key Evidence Focus:** Morale, anxiety, role clarity, perceptions of leadership credibility, turnover trends
- **Data Availability:** Moderate, often anecdotal but present in case studies and HR analyses

#### Other Internal Groups
- **Evidence Source:** HR integration teams, unions/works councils (where applicable), analyst reports on workforce impact
- **Key Evidence Focus:** Operational bottlenecks, onboarding effectiveness, retention strategies, escalation of employee concerns.
- **Data Availability:** Moderate, varies by industry and reporting transparency

### External Stakeholders
#### Customers/Clients
- **Evidence Source:** Customer satisfaction surveys published in case studies, analyst reports, media coverage of customer reactions
- **Key Evidence Focus:** Continuity of service, product quality, trust in brand post-acquisition
- **Data Availability:** Moderate, often reported in industry analysis and consumer press

#### Partners/Suppliers
- **Evidence Source:** Supplier interviews in trade publications, analyst commentary on supply chain impacts
- **Key Evidence Focus:** Contract stability, collaboration continuity, concerns about integration risks
- **Data Availability:** Limited, usually anecdotal or inferred from industry commentary

## Data Collection Methods

### Evidence Collection
#### Evidence Sources
- Company Publications: Annual reports, press releases, leadership statements
- News & Media: Coverage of employee, customer, and investor reactions
- Analyst Reports: Industry commentary on integration outcomes and stakeholder impacts
- Academic Case Studies: Documented stakeholder perspectives in published research
- Practitioner Interviews: HR leaders and integration specialists reflecting on stakeholder reactions

#### Evidence Extraction
- Problem Assessment: Identify stakeholder concerns (e.g., morale, trust, service continuity) documented in sources
- Solution Feedback: Capture stakeholder responses to integration strategies (e.g., communication, retention programs)
- Priority & Support: Note stakeholder emphasis on what mattered most (e.g., culture, pricing, autonomy)

## Sampling Strategy

### Stakeholder Identification
#### Stakeholder Mapping
- Identify which groups are represented in existing sources (employees, leadership, customers, investors, suppliers).

#### Representation Goals
- Ensure coverage across internal and external groups, even if uneven in depth.

#### Limitations
- Acknowledge missing voices (e.g., frontline staff often underrepresented in published data)

## Ethical Considerations

### Citation Integrity
- Always cite sources transparently (annual reports, interviews, case studies).

### Confidentiality
- Use only publicly available data; avoid private or unpublished employee commentary.

### Bias Awareness
- Recognize that company publications are self-reported and may understate problems.

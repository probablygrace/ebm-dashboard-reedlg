## PICOC Framework
### Population (Who is affected by this problem?)
Employees in mid- to large-sized companies undergoing M&As.

### Intervention (What solution/action are we considering?)
Cultural interation strategies, including Town halls (monthly all hands with Q&A), Cascading messages (weekly leadership updates to direct reports), Skip-level meetings (quarterly CEO sessions with frontline), Leader training (4-hour culturalintegration workshop covering resistance management, uncertainty communication, values alignment messaging).

### Comparison (What alternatives exist?)
No formal or planned cultural integration plan.

### Outcomes (What will success look like?)
Increased employee engagement, trust, and organizational commitment, as well as stabilized perceived job security.

### Context (Where/when does this apply?)
Transition phase during company M&As.

## Clear Problem Statement
Company culture is susceptible to deterioration during mergers and acquisitions (M&As), becoming more negative and fragile due to misaligned values, unclear communication, and employee uncertainty.

## Why This Problem Matters
### Impact on Organization
Cultural integration during mergers and acquisitions should be a top priority for organizational stability and long-term success. Without a structured integration plan, indicators such as engagement, turnover, and satisfaction ratings risk worsening, creating further instability across the organization.

### Stakeholder Impact
A flawed integration can lead to worsened business performance influenced by the dysfunctional work environment, negatively impacting financial performance and the overall stability and profitability of the business(es).

### Strategic Alignment
Successful cultural integration should be a top priority during M&As because it directly impacts company viability. It should not be assumed that the cultures will naturally integrate or that the cultures are "compatible enough" not to need a formalized integration plan.
